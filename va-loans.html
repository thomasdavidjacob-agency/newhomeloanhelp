VA Loan Benefits — Simply Explained

The VA loan program rewards your service with benefits that most home buyers never get access to.

🏦
$0 Down Payment

No down payment required for eligible borrowers with full entitlement. Buy a home with nothing out of pocket.

🚫
No PMI

Private mortgage insurance is never required on VA loans — saving you hundreds of dollars every month.

📉
Competitive Rates

VA loans typically carry lower interest rates than conventional financing, reducing your payment for the life of the loan.

🔄
Reusable Benefit

Your VA entitlement doesn't expire. Use it, pay it off, and use it again as many times as you qualify.

📋
Flexible Qualifying

VA guidelines are more lenient than conventional loans. Manual underwriting available for complex credit histories.

🛡
Limited Closing Costs

The VA restricts the fees lenders can charge veterans at closing, keeping your out-of-pocket costs low.

Who Qualifies for a VA Loan?

VA loans are available to a broad range of service members, veterans, and their families. Here's a simple overview:

  • ✓
    Veterans: 90 consecutive days of active service during wartime, or 181 days during peacetime
  • ✓
    Active Duty: Currently serving with 90+ days of continuous active service
  • ✓
    National Guard / Reserves: 6 years of service, or 90 days active under Title 10 or Title 32 orders
  • ✓
    Surviving Spouses: Un-remarried spouses of veterans who died in service or from a service-connected disability
  • ✓
    Credit: No VA minimum; most lenders prefer 580+; manual underwriting available

VA Loan FAQ

Do VA loans require a down payment?
No. Eligible veterans with full VA entitlement can purchase a home anywhere in Oregon with $0 down.
Are there VA loan limits?
Since 2020, VA loan limits were eliminated for borrowers with full entitlement. You can borrow what your income and credit support.
Can I use my VA loan benefit more than once?
Yes. VA benefits are reusable. As long as you have remaining entitlement, you can use the benefit again.
What credit score do I need for a VA loan?
The VA itself sets no minimum. Most lenders prefer 580+. I specialize in manual underwriting for complex credit situations.
What is the VA Funding Fee?
A one-time fee of 1.25%–3.3% depending on your loan. Veterans with a service-connected disability rating are exempt.
